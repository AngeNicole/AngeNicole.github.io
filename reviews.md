# Book Reviews

Honest, student-written reviews of books I have read recently. Each review includes my personal take and a rating out of five stars.

---

</div>

<div style="max-width:800px">

<div class="review-card">
  <h2 style="font-size:1.25rem;font-weight:600;color:#0f172a;margin:0 0 0.25rem">To Kill a Mockingbird — Harper Lee</h2>
  <p class="review-rating">★★★★★ 5.0</p>
  <p style="color:#475569;margin-top:0.75rem">This book stayed with me long after I finished it. Harper Lee's writing is warm and accessible, but the themes are profound. Atticus Finch is one of the greatest characters in literature — a moral compass in a world full of prejudice. I recommend it to anyone, whether you are reading it for the first time or revisiting it.</p>
  <p class="review-who">Who should read this: Everyone. It is timeless.</p>
</div>

<div class="review-card">
  <h2 style="font-size:1.25rem;font-weight:600;color:#0f172a;margin:0 0 0.25rem">Sapiens — Yuval Noah Harari</h2>
  <p class="review-rating">★★★★½ 4.5</p>
  <p style="color:#475569;margin-top:0.75rem">Sapiens completely changed how I think about history. Harari does not just list dates and events — he asks big questions about why humans dominate the planet. Some of his arguments are controversial, which makes it even more interesting to read. The chapters on the Agricultural Revolution and the role of shared myths were my favorites.</p>
  <p class="review-who">Who should read this: Anyone curious about the big picture of human civilization.</p>
</div>

<div class="review-card">
  <h2 style="font-size:1.25rem;font-weight:600;color:#0f172a;margin:0 0 0.25rem">Atomic Habits — James Clear</h2>
  <p class="review-rating">★★★★½ 4.5</p>
  <p style="color:#475569;margin-top:0.75rem">As a student, I found this book incredibly useful. The concept of making small, incremental improvements is simple but powerful. I started applying some of the strategies right away — like habit stacking and environment design. It is not just about productivity; it is about becoming the kind of person you want to be.</p>
  <p class="review-who">Who should read this: Students, professionals, or anyone who wants to build better daily routines.</p>
</div>

<div class="review-card">
  <h2 style="font-size:1.25rem;font-weight:600;color:#0f172a;margin:0 0 0.25rem">1984 — George Orwell</h2>
  <p class="review-rating">★★★★★ 5.0</p>
  <p style="color:#475569;margin-top:0.75rem">Reading 1984 felt unsettling in the best way. Orwell's world of constant surveillance, propaganda, and thought control is terrifying because it does not feel entirely fictional. The story of Winston Smith's quiet rebellion is heartbreaking and thought-provoking. This is one of those books that makes you pay closer attention to the world around you.</p>
  <p class="review-who">Who should read this: Anyone interested in politics, freedom, or dystopian fiction.</p>
</div>

<div class="review-card">
  <h2 style="font-size:1.25rem;font-weight:600;color:#0f172a;margin:0 0 0.25rem">The Alchemist — Paulo Coelho</h2>
  <p class="review-rating">★★★★ 4.0</p>
  <p style="color:#475569;margin-top:0.75rem">The Alchemist is a quick, beautiful read. The language is simple, almost like a fable, but the message runs deep. It reminded me that the journey matters as much as the destination. It is the kind of book you finish in an afternoon and think about for weeks.</p>
  <p class="review-who">Who should read this: Anyone looking for an uplifting, reflective read.</p>
</div>

<div class="review-card">
  <h2 style="font-size:1.25rem;font-weight:600;color:#0f172a;margin:0 0 0.25rem">Dune — Frank Herbert</h2>
  <p class="review-rating">★★★★★ 5.0</p>
  <p style="color:#475569;margin-top:0.75rem">Dune is one of the most ambitious novels I have ever read. The world-building is extraordinary — Herbert thought through everything from the ecology of the desert to the politics of spice trading. It takes a bit of patience to get into, but once you are hooked, it is impossible to put down.</p>
  <p class="review-who">Who should read this: Fans of science fiction, complex plots, and richly imagined worlds.</p>
</div>
