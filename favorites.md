# Favorite Books

A handpicked selection of books that I have enjoyed and would recommend to any reader. These span multiple genres — from timeless classics to modern bestsellers.

---

## 1. To Kill a Mockingbird
**Author:** Harper Lee
**Genre:** Classic Fiction
**Rating:** ★★★★★

A powerful story about racial injustice and moral growth in the American South. Told through the eyes of young Scout Finch, this novel remains one of the most important works of American literature. It teaches empathy, courage, and the importance of standing up for what is right.

---

## 2. Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind
**Author:** Yuval Noah Harari
**Genre:** Non-Fiction / History
**Rating:** ★★★★½

A sweeping overview of human history — from the Stone Age to the Silicon Age. Harari explores how biology, culture, and technology shaped the world we live in today. It challenges you to think about where humanity is headed next.

---

## 3. The Hobbit
**Author:** J.R.R. Tolkien
**Genre:** Fantasy
**Rating:** ★★★★★

The adventure of Bilbo Baggins, a quiet hobbit who is swept into an epic quest to reclaim a lost kingdom. Full of charm, wit, and unforgettable characters, this is the perfect introduction to Tolkien's Middle-earth.

---

## 4. Educated
**Author:** Tara Westover
**Genre:** Memoir
**Rating:** ★★★★½

A remarkable memoir about a woman who grew up in a survivalist family in Idaho and went on to earn a PhD from Cambridge University. It is a story about the transformative power of education and the courage it takes to reinvent yourself.

---

## 5. 1984
**Author:** George Orwell
**Genre:** Dystopian Fiction
**Rating:** ★★★★★

Orwell's chilling vision of a totalitarian future where independent thought is a crime. Decades after its publication, 1984 remains terrifyingly relevant. It is essential reading for anyone who cares about freedom, privacy, and truth.

---

## 6. Atomic Habits
**Author:** James Clear
**Genre:** Self-Help / Productivity
**Rating:** ★★★★½

A practical guide to building good habits and breaking bad ones. Clear explains the science behind habit formation and offers simple strategies that anyone can apply. Whether you want to read more, exercise regularly, or study effectively, this book gives you the tools.

---

## 7. The Alchemist
**Author:** Paulo Coelho
**Genre:** Fiction / Philosophy
**Rating:** ★★★★

A short, inspiring novel about a shepherd boy who travels from Spain to Egypt in search of a treasure. Along the way, he discovers that the real treasure is the journey itself. It is a story about following your dreams and listening to your heart.

---

## 8. Dune
**Author:** Frank Herbert
**Genre:** Science Fiction
**Rating:** ★★★★★

An epic tale of politics, religion, and ecology set on the desert planet Arrakis. Herbert created one of the most richly detailed worlds in all of science fiction. If you enjoy complex world-building and thought-provoking themes, Dune is a must-read.
