# Book Genres

One of the best features of the original Book Hub app is browsing books by genre. Here is an overview of the genres featured on the site, along with recommended titles for each.

---

## Classic Fiction

Timeless novels that have shaped literature and continue to resonate with readers today.

- **To Kill a Mockingbird** — Harper Lee
- **Pride and Prejudice** — Jane Austen
- **The Great Gatsby** — F. Scott Fitzgerald
- **Jane Eyre** — Charlotte Brontë

---

## Fantasy

Imaginative worlds, epic quests, and unforgettable characters.

- **The Hobbit** — J.R.R. Tolkien
- **Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone** — J.K. Rowling
- **A Game of Thrones** — George R.R. Martin
- **The Name of the Wind** — Patrick Rothfuss

---

## Science Fiction

Thought-provoking stories about technology, space, and the future of humanity.

- **Dune** — Frank Herbert
- **Ender's Game** — Orson Scott Card
- **The Martian** — Andy Weir
- **Neuromancer** — William Gibson

---

## Non-Fiction

Real stories, big ideas, and explorations of the world around us.

- **Sapiens** — Yuval Noah Harari
- **Educated** — Tara Westover
- **Thinking, Fast and Slow** — Daniel Kahneman
- **The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks** — Rebecca Skloot

---

## Self-Help & Productivity

Practical advice for personal growth, habits, and effectiveness.

- **Atomic Habits** — James Clear
- **Deep Work** — Cal Newport
- **The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People** — Stephen Covey
- **How to Win Friends and Influence People** — Dale Carnegie

---

## Dystopian Fiction

Dark visions of society that warn us about the dangers of unchecked power.

- **1984** — George Orwell
- **Brave New World** — Aldous Huxley
- **Fahrenheit 451** — Ray Bradbury
- **The Handmaid's Tale** — Margaret Atwood

---

## Mystery & Thriller

Page-turners full of suspense, twists, and detective work.

- **Gone Girl** — Gillian Flynn
- **The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo** — Stieg Larsson
- **And Then There Were None** — Agatha Christie
- **The Da Vinci Code** — Dan Brown

---

## Philosophy & Fiction

Stories that explore deep questions about meaning, identity, and existence.

- **The Alchemist** — Paulo Coelho
- **Siddhartha** — Hermann Hesse
- **The Stranger** — Albert Camus
- **Sophie's World** — Jostein Gaarder
